# Sector 01 — Fire & Rescue (UK)

*The home sector. The most demanding to win (XVR/LearnPro owns it) but the one where the competency framework is best understood and most rigid — which makes the competency→content mapping cleanest. Use this as the reference template for every other sector.*

## 1. Snapshot
- **Target universe:** 49 UK fire & rescue services (44 England · 1 Scotland · 3 Wales · 1 NI), plus the Fire Service College, regional training hubs, and on-station/whole-time training teams.
- **Procurement:** Public sector — slow, framework-driven. *(Note the conflict-of-interest caution if selling to your own employer.)*
- **Buyer / contact:** Head of Training & Development · Incident Command Lead / Command & Control User Group rep · Learning & Organisational Development · Station/Group Commanders running maintenance-of-competence.
- **Why it still matters despite the incumbent:** the day-to-day TDE workflow (PowerPoint + facilitator) is exactly what Command360 replaces, cheaply and without hardware. Win on the routine, high-frequency exercising the big VR suite is overkill for.

## 2. The mandate (the rigid guidelines)
UK fire command training is governed by a tight, published stack — this is the hook:
- **NFCC National Operational Guidance (NOG)** — the national doctrine for Incident Command and Operations. Services must base policy, procedure and training on it and evidence alignment.
- **Fire Standards Board — Operational Competence & Operational Response standards** — services must maintain, assess and *evidence* the competence of operational and control personnel; inspected by **HMICFRS**.
- **The four command levels:** Level 1 (operational/initial), Level 2 (tactical), Level 3 (advanced tactical / Silver), Level 4 (strategic / Gold).
- **JESIP** — Joint Emergency Services Interoperability Principles for multi-agency incidents (see §3).
- **National Operational Learning (NOL)** — continuous-improvement loop feeding new/emerging risk into training.
- **Maintenance of competence** is recurring and auditable — commanders must re-practise and be re-assessed. *That recurring requirement is the recurring-revenue hook.*

## 3. Competencies to develop (worked backwards from the mandate)
What a fire incident commander must be assessed against — each is a content target:
- **Incident command system application** — structuring, organising and managing an incident at the right level; command transition/handover; span of control.
- **Dynamic Risk Assessment (DRA)** and analytical risk assessment — continuous hazard/risk evaluation; UKFRS risk philosophy; operational discretion.
- **Decision-making under pressure** — recognition-primed (naturalistic) and analytical decision-making; the decision-control process.
- **Tactical mode** — offensive/defensive/transitional and communicating it.
- **Command skills (THINCS behavioural markers)** — situational awareness, decision-making, leadership, communication, teamworking, personal resilience. (THINCS is referenced in NOG and used by a number of UK services and airport fire services — a ready-made assessment vocabulary to map our objectives onto.)
- **Multi-agency / JESIP** — the five principles (co-locate, communicate, co-ordinate, jointly understand risk, shared situational awareness), the **Joint Decision Model (JDM)**, **METHANE/ETHANE** messaging, **IIMARCH** briefing, Tactical/Strategic Co-ordinating Groups (TCG/SCG).
- **Resource deployment & escalation**, sectorisation, crew safety & accountability (BA entry control).
- **Post-incident review & organisational learning** (NOL).

## 4. What they train on (scenarios)
Structural fires (residential, high-rise, commercial) · wildfire / rural · HAZMAT · RTC / extrication · **BESS (battery energy storage) fires** · industrial & warehouse fires · water/flooding & USAR · fire investigation · control-room / dispatch · major-incident and multi-agency command.

## 5. How Command360 helps (competency → capability — SEO copy seed)
- **Command decision-making** → live, branching scenarios where the commander commits resources and the scene evolves with their choices; recognition-primed practice at volume, any room, no hardware.
- **Dynamic Risk Assessment** → injects that reveal new hazards mid-incident, forcing reassessment; hotspots to identify risks on the scene image.
- **Tactical mode & sectorisation** → place and re-task resources on a 2D incident ground; capture the declared mode.
- **JESIP / multi-agency** → multi-participant live sessions that bring police/ambulance/utilities into the same exercise remotely; a METHANE/JDM-structured decision log captured automatically.
- **Command skills assessment (THINCS-aligned)** → objectives tagged to command-skill markers; assessor view; performance captured per participant.
- **Maintenance of competence** → reusable scenario library + auto-generated after-action record = auditable evidence for the Fire Standards / HMICFRS.

## 6. Asset library required (`library/fire/`)
- `backplate/` — residential street, terraced house, high-rise exterior & floor, commercial unit, warehouse interior, industrial yard, motorway/RTC, rural/wildfire, BESS/substation, town centre, night/rain variants.
- `hazard/` — flame (window/roof/internal), smoke (light→thick, colour-coded), backdraught/flashover cues, structural collapse, fuel/chemical spill, gas cylinder, electrical arc, flood water.
- `casualty/` — trapped/visible casualties (window, vehicle, ground), varying severity, BA-team rescue.
- `asset/` — appliances (pump, aerial/ALP, command unit), hose lines, BA sets, RTC tooling, cordons, water supply.
- `responder/` — firefighters (BA-rig, command role tabards), partner agencies (police, ambulance, utilities).
- `prop/` — command board markers, sector/cordon signage, METHANE/JDM overlays, hazard placards (HAZMAT diamonds, ADR).
- `effect/` — flame loops, smoke drift, blue-light flicker, water spray, sparks. *(Reused across all sectors.)*

## 7. Training landscape (providers, awarding bodies, courses)
- **Awarding bodies:** SFJ Awards (Incident Command Levels 3/4/6/7 + revalidation; used by ~2/3 of FRSs) · Pearson Edexcel (Level 3/4/6 Incident Command awards).
- **Delivery:** The Fire Service College (Moreton-in-Marsh) · in-service command training teams · Institution of Fire Engineers (IFE) syllabus & CPD.
- **Assessment tools:** THINCS (THe INcident Command Skills) behavioural-marker system, NFCC-owned.
- **Incumbent simulation:** XVR On Scene (3D VR), RiVR (video). *Command360's lane: low-cost, browser-based, high-frequency TDE delivery + multi-agency.*
