# Command360 — Compliance & Certification Costs

*The certification ladder with real 2026 UK prices, sequenced by cost and leverage. The good news: the things that actually open doors are cheap. As a solo founder you're in the **micro tier** for almost everything, which is the lowest price band.*

> **Caveat:** figures are 2026 estimates that change; confirm with the provider before paying. Not legal or financial advice — have a solicitor review the document templates before use.

## 2. What's mandatory vs what gates deals
- **Mandatory by law (do regardless):** the **ICO fee** (£52/yr) — you process personal data, so this isn't optional.
- **Gates the public market:** **Cyber Essentials** — it's the minimum requirement for **G-Cloud Lot 2 (cloud software)**, your fast route to every blue-light and public buyer. This one ~£320 spend unlocks the biggest channel.
- **Gates specific buyers:** **NHS DSPT** (free) for health; **WCAG 2.2 AA** for any public body (legal); **ISO 27001 / CE Plus** for the largest, most security-conscious, or defence buyers.
- **Protects you:** **insurance** + the **ToS training-aid disclaimer** (see the templates) — essential before putting the tool in front of life-safety organisations.

## 3. The cheapest sensible route (and the smart sequence)
1. **ICO fee — £52.** Do it this week; it's the law and trivially cheap.
2. **Cyber Essentials, self-certified — ~£320.** Self-cert is the cheapest route; use the free NCSC readiness guides and the free IASME questionnaire. Bonus: certifying the whole org gives you **free £25,000 cyber-liability insurance** (UK orgs under £20m turnover). This single step opens G-Cloud.
3. **Accessibility — start free.** Run automated tools (axe, Lighthouse, WAVE) and fix what they flag; commission a paid audit (~£1–5k) only when you're scaling into public bodies.
4. **Insurance — ~£300–£1,000/yr.** Get PI + product liability before the first paid deployment.
5. **Solicitor review — ~£500–£1,500.** Have the DPA, ToS and Privacy Notice templates reviewed before you sign anything.
6. **Defer the expensive ones.** CE Plus, ISO 27001 and pen tests only when a specific deal or buyer demands them — don't spend £10k+ on ISO before a customer needs it. ISO's ROI is usually a single large contract; CE first reduces its scope later.
7. **NHS DSPT (free)** when you open the ambulance/health front.

## 4. Year-one budget snapshot
**Realistic minimum to be market-ready as a solo founder:**
- ICO £52 + Cyber Essentials ~£320 + accessibility (DIY, ~£0) + insurance ~£500 + solicitor review ~£1,000 ≈ **£1,900–£3,400 all-in.**

Everything above that (CE Plus, ISO 27001, pen test, paid accessibility audit, NHS DSPT effort) is **deal-driven** — spend it when a customer or channel requires it, funded by the revenue it unlocks. The headline for your pitch and your plan: *you can be fully market-ready in the public sector for low single-thousands, not tens of thousands.*

## 5. Note on tax & funding
- These are allowable business expenses (effective relief ~19–25%).
- Some can be offset by non-dilutive funding (Innovate UK, R&D tax relief, DASA for the defence angle) — worth exploring so the security/compliance build doesn't come out of cash flow.
