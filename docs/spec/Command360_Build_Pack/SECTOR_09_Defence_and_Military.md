# Sector 09 — Defence & Military

*A specialised, relationship-driven public buyer with its own regulator (DSA) that mirrors the civilian fire frameworks closely — Defence ARFF regulations track CAP 168/699, and Defence references NFCC NOG and JESIP directly. Slower MOD procurement, but a coherent, mandated maintenance-of-competence regime and a natural extension of the aviation and fire content.*

---

## 1. Snapshot
- **Target universe:** Defence Fire & Rescue across the MOD estate (RAF, Army and Royal Navy bases, ranges, fuel and explosives sites); defence aerodromes (e.g. RAF Brize Norton); the defence training estate; service police (RMP/RNP/RAFP); defence nuclear (DNSR); Defence Medical Services.
- **Procurement:** **Public / MOD** — slower and relationship-driven, often via Front Line Commands or prime contractors delivering defence fire services.
- **Buyer / contact:** Front Line Command training leads · Station Fire Officer · Defence Fire training centre · training establishment SQEP · contingency-plans staff.
- **Edge:** Defence runs a continuous maintenance-of-competence regime with few live incidents and dispersed sites; a browser command-exercise tool that aligns to the Defence regulations and produces proficiency evidence fits the model — and reuses the aviation/fire asset libraries.

## 2. The mandate (the rigid guidelines)
- **Defence Safety Authority (DSA)** — the independent regulator and investigator for health, safety and environmental protection in Defence (includes the Defence Accident Investigation Branch).
- **Defence Fire Safety Regulator (DFSR)** — enforces UK fire legislation on MOD premises across **Aerodrome Rescue & Fire Fighting (ARFF)**, **Structural Fire Fighting**, **Major Accident Control Regulations** and military aviation.
- **DSA02 DFSR regulations:** the **Defence ARFF Regulations** (mirror CAP 168/699 and ICAO Annex 14; 15 aircraft categories) and the **Defence Structural Fire Fighting Regulations** — both require incident-command competence and a tested response.
- **Defence Systems Approach to Training (DSAT)** under **JSP 822**, with a **Maintenance of Competence Scheme (MOCS)**, a **Training Requirements Authority (TRA)** and **proficiency checks** at TRA-set intervals; records endorsed by the TRA.
- **NATO STANAGs:** 7145 (minimum core competency for firefighters), **7193 / AATMP-44 (Incident Command System for fire & emergency services)**, and the CFR series; **TACEVAL** evaluation for deployed/operational training.
- Defence also references **NFCC National Operational Guidance** and **JESIP** for civil-emergency interoperability.

## 3. Competencies to develop (worked backwards from DSA02/JSP 822)
- **Aircraft & structural incident command** — command within the aerodrome response standard; structural/domestic firefighting on the estate.
- **Major Incident Plan / CONPLAN activation** — coordinating station resources and civilian emergency services for a major accident.
- **Aircraft fire & rescue** — fuel fires, polymer-composite hazards, rescue-path creation, mass rescue.
- **Major Accident Control** — fuel and explosives incidents on the estate.
- **Multi-agency working with civilian emergency services** — JESIP-aligned, off-base coordination.
- **Deployed / operational firefighting** — austere-environment response (STANAG-aligned).
- **Maintenance of competence** — TRA-set proficiency, recorded and endorsed (MOCS).

## 4. What they train on (scenarios)
Air-system crash (on or near the aerodrome) · aircraft ground fire · hangar / building fire · fuel or explosives incident (Major Accident Control) · structural collapse · RTC on the estate · range incident · multi-agency major incident with civilian services · deployed-operations response.
