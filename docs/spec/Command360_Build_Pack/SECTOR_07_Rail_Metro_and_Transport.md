# Sector 07 — Rail, Metro & Transport

*A safety-critical sector where emergency management is formally the "last layer of protection" and joint exercising is explicitly encouraged by the regulator. Mix of public (Network Rail / Great British Railways) and private operators. XVR already sells here (Network Rail, ProRail, LA Metro, Westerschelde Tunnel).*

---

## 1. Snapshot
- **Target universe:** Network Rail; passenger and freight train operators; Transport for London / London Underground and other light-rail & metro systems (Tyne & Wear, Manchester Metrolink, Glasgow Subway, etc.); HS1/HS2; Eurotunnel/Channel Tunnel.
- **Procurement:** Mixed — Network Rail/GBR public; operators and metros run their own (some private). Generally faster than central-government tendering.
- **Buyer / contact:** Emergency Planning / Resilience Manager · Rail Incident Officer (RIO) lead · Station / Control safety · Competence Manager · (metro) Duty Operations Manager.
- **Edge:** rail runs scenario-based joint exercises and learns formally from investigations; a browser tool that rehearses station, tunnel and derailment command — and captures the learning — fits the regulator's direction without closing a line.

## 2. The mandate (the rigid guidelines)
- **Office of Rail and Road (ORR)** — the health & safety regulator; railways hold a **Safety Authorisation / Safety Certificate** and run a **Safety Management System (SMS)**; emergency management is part of the SMS and the **"last layer of protection"** against escalation.
- **Rail Delivery Group (RDG) Approved Codes of Practice / Guidance Notes** on Rail / Integrated Emergency Management, arising from the industry **Rail Resilience Project (RRP)**.
- **Civil Contingencies Act 2004** underpins multi-agency planning.
- **RAIB (Rail Accident Investigation Branch)** — independent investigations whose reports and "summaries of learning" feed competence; studying investigation reports is a recognised training input.
- **Station & tunnel fire safety** — BS 9992 and Network Rail/LU standards (QRA, HAZID); Channel Tunnel governed by the Intergovernmental Commission and Channel Tunnel Safety Authority.
- **Stranded-passenger management** — updated guidance (GN49) after recurring incidents; the ORR explicitly promotes resilience through **scenario planning and joint exercises**.

## 3. Competencies to develop (worked backwards from the SMS/ACoP)
- **Rail incident command** — the **Rail Incident Officer** role; initial response, escalation, command transition.
- **Emergency-plan activation** — operator/Network Rail plans, control and signalling response, isolation/detonator protection.
- **Station, tunnel & train evacuation management** — crowd control, platform-train interface ("trap and drag"), tunnel walkways.
- **Multi-agency / JESIP** — joint working with fire, police (incl. BTP) and ambulance.
- **Stranded-train / passenger management** (GN49) — welfare, vulnerable passengers, communications.
- **Learning from investigations** — applying RAIB recommendations.
- **Business continuity** — service recovery, asset and security management.

## 4. What they train on (scenarios)
Derailment · train collision · station incident (fire, crowd, platform-train interface) · tunnel fire & evacuation · level-crossing incident · stranded train / passenger welfare · overspeeding · trespass / fatality · dangerous-goods (freight) incident.
