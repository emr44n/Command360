# Command360 — Go-to-Market & Outreach

*The "how it actually gets bought" layer, combining Imran's 20 years inside a UK fire service with the current procurement and outreach rules. The headline: at ~£10k/site you sit far below the tender thresholds, so most sales can be direct-awarded — which means relationships and pre-engagement, not formal bids, win the work.*

> **Caveat:** procurement thresholds, framework dates and outreach rules change. Verify the specifics before acting, and take legal/procurement advice for anything contractual. Not legal advice.

---

## 1. The procurement reality (use this to your advantage)
**Below-threshold = buyer discretion.** From 1 January 2026 the public-procurement thresholds are roughly £207,720 for sub-central bodies (councils, NHS) and £135,018 for central government. A ~£10k site licence sits *far* below these. Below threshold, buyers have wide discretion: they don't need a full OJEU-style competitive tender. This is exactly the world Imran describes — where a buyer who wants a particular tool can shape the requirement around it. **Translation: get in early, build the relationship, and help shape the spec before anything goes out.**

**G-Cloud is the fast lane.** Command360 is cloud software → **G-Cloud Lot 2 (Cloud software)** on the Crown Commercial Service / GCA framework lets public bodies — explicitly including blue light (police, fire, ambulance, search & rescue) — buy from the catalogue by **direct award, no full tender**. The entry requirement for Lot 2 is **Cyber Essentials** (see the data/privacy file). The current framework (G-Cloud 14) runs to late October 2026; **G-Cloud 15** is an *open framework* under the Procurement Act 2023, going live around September 2026 and **reopening periodically for new suppliers** — so a startup isn't locked out for years. *Getting listed on G-Cloud is the single highest-leverage admin task to remove friction in the public market.*

**Where competition does kick in.** Contracts over ~£30k must be advertised on Contracts Finder; multi-site/national deals (e.g. a single national service) can cross the threshold into a fuller process. So price the entry-level single-site deals to stay in direct-award territory, and treat national deals as proper, planned bids.

**"Quality and cheapest" is your friend.** UK public tenders weight on most-economically-advantageous (quality + price). Imran's read from the inside: price matters a lot, and Command360 undercuts VR dramatically. You win the price axis outright and compete on fit — so lead with "solves your exact problem, at a fraction of xVR's cost, with the evidence pack the regulator wants."

## 2. Fire first — the bridgehead (Imran's relationships)
- **Start with fire**, where Imran has two decades of relationships and credibility. It's also the market with the **most buyers** and they are **disjointed** — separate authorities that all use NFCC National Operational Guidance as a baseline but each configure and run it slightly differently.
- **That disjointedness is a product requirement:** a configurable platform that respects local variation (each service's own scenarios, terminology, branding) beats a one-size tool. Sell "your NOG, your way," not "our way."
- **Fire Service College contrast:** widely known but seen as expensive and not especially highly rated — useful positioning. Command360 is cheaper, runs in-house, any day, no travel.
- **Budgets:** local-authority money is tight but real, and services do receive training funding. The pitch is *making their life easier and solving their problem cheaply*, not a premium product.

## 4. Outreach tooling & costs (register free, pay small where it earns its keep)
- **Free, high-trust (do this first):** LinkedIn free + Imran's existing fire network. For the fire bridgehead, warm intros beat any tool — this costs nothing and converts best.
- **LinkedIn Sales Navigator Core** — roughly £70–90/month (about £900/year on annual billing), plus VAT; gives 40+ search filters, lead lists, alerts and 50 InMails/month. Worth it once you're prospecting beyond your own network. *(It does not export emails — pair with an email-finder if you want to email.)*
- **CRM:** start on a free tier (e.g. HubSpot free) to track contacts, conversations and opt-outs (the suppression list is a compliance requirement, not just hygiene).
- **Email-finder / verification** (small monthly fee) only if you run email outreach alongside LinkedIn.
- **Lean stack total:** effectively £0 to start (network + free LinkedIn + free CRM), scaling to ~£100–150/month once Sales Navigator + a finder are added. Break-even is one extra deal.

## 5. Outreach compliance basics (UK)
UK B2B outreach to your targets is **lawful** — they're almost all corporate/public bodies:
- **PECR corporate-subscriber exemption:** you can send unsolicited B2B marketing email to **limited companies, LLPs, Scottish partnerships and public bodies** (which covers fire/police/NHS/ports authorities) **without prior consent**, provided you identify yourself and include a working opt-out.
- **UK GDPR still applies** to the named individual: you need a lawful basis — for prospecting this is **legitimate interest**, which the ICO recognises and which is now formally recognised in the Data (Use and Access) Act 2025. Do a short **Legitimate Interests Assessment** (ICO publishes a template), keep it on file, and be transparent (who you are, why, how you got their details, how to opt out).
- **Watch-outs:** sole traders and unincorporated partnerships (England/Wales/NI) and personal/freemail addresses are treated as individuals → need consent. Don't buy lists (broker consent doesn't transfer). Maintain a suppression list and act on opt-outs immediately. ICO fines are steep (PECR up to £500k, can hit directors).
- **LinkedIn** is governed by its own platform rules (connection requests + InMail); the same GDPR transparency principles apply, but it sidesteps the email question entirely — which is why LinkedIn-first suits a relationship-led founder.
