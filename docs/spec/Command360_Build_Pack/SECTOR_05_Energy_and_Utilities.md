# Sector 05 — Energy & Utilities

*A broad private/regulated pool with heavy COMAH overlap (Sector 06) plus its own sector-specific emergency-exercise mandates. Nuclear and offshore have the hardest, most documented exercise regimes; networks and water sit under sector-resilience direction. Mostly private and fast-moving (nuclear/defence the slower exception). XVR already sells here (Phillips 66, npower, Enexis).*

## 1. Snapshot
- **Target universe:** the nuclear estate (operating stations, Sellafield, new build at Hinkley/Sizewell, plus defence nuclear); offshore oil, gas and renewables (platforms and wind farms); electricity & gas networks (National Grid, distribution operators, substations); the water companies; many of these sites are *also* COMAH (see Sector 06).
- **Procurement:** Mostly **private** and direct; nuclear and defence are slower and assurance-heavy.
- **Buyer / contact:** Emergency Planning / Response Manager · Control Room Manager · HSE / Process Safety · Resilience Lead · (offshore) Emergency Response team / OIM training.
- **Edge:** these operators run mandated, recurring exercises (REPPIR, triennial offshore, network resilience) and lean heavily on control-room command — a browser tool runs and evidences them cheaply.

## 2. The mandate (the rigid guidelines)
Sector-specific regimes stack on top of COMAH:
- **Nuclear:** the **Office for Nuclear Regulation (ONR)** regulates emergency preparedness; **REPPIR 2019** (Radiation Emergency Preparedness and Public Information Regulations) requires on-site and off-site emergency plans that must be **exercised and tested**; the **safety case** (ONR Safety Assessment Principles) underpins everything; defence nuclear adds the Defence Nuclear Safety Regulator.
- **Offshore:** **OEUK** guidance and the **OEER** collaboration (EPOL, OREEF); the **Integrated Offshore Emergency Response (IOER)** guidelines with HM Coastguard for SAR; **TRIREX (Triennial Renewables Exercise)** — a large multi-agency exercise every three years, run live with a parallel **table-top** and **remote observers**.
- **Utilities / networks / water:** the **Security & Emergency Measures Direction (SEMD)** places emergency-response duties on operators; **Civil Contingencies Act 2004** applies; **National Power Outage (NPO)** planning; control rooms are central.
- Common thread across all three: control-room command, the safety case / risk basis, multi-agency exercising, and business continuity.

## 3. Competencies to develop (worked backwards from the above)
- **On-site & control-room incident command** — initial response, escalation, command of site/network emergency teams.
- **Emergency-plan activation** — on-site then off-site/external plan; mobilising mutual aid and blue-light services.
- **Release / radiation / network-fault management** — recognising and containing radiological, gas, electrical and process events.
- **Multi-agency & SAR coordination** — with fire/police/ambulance, HM Coastguard, the LRF/off-site authority.
- **Consequence management** — plume/contamination/outage spread, shelter-vs-evacuate, public protection.
- **Business continuity & network resilience**, and recovery.

## 4. What they train on (scenarios)
Substation / transformer fire · battery storage (BESS) fire · gas release & explosion · nuclear site emergency / REPPIR exercise · offshore platform fire or blowout · wind-turbine / offshore incident with SAR · network or national power outage · water-treatment failure / contamination · control-room exercise.
