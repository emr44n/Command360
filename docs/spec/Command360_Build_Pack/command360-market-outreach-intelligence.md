# Command360 — Market & Outreach Intelligence

**Purpose:** A working prospect map for reaching out to potential clients. It catalogues every sector XVR / LearnPro Group sells into, the named organisations they already serve (proof that budget and need exist), the UK universe of targets in each sector, the regulatory reason each buyer *has* to train, who to contact, and how to pitch Command360 as the cheaper, browser-based alternative.

**How to use this:** Sections 3 and 4 are the live target list — start there. Section 5 tells you *why* each target must buy (the regulatory hook to lead with). Appendix A is the product side: what specialisms to build into the platform to serve each vertical.

*Compiled from a full read of the XVR site (learnprogroup.com/xvr) — all four sector pages and both Our Users pages — plus UK regulatory and market sources. Last updated: June 2026. Client names are XVR's publicly listed customers; treat counts as planning estimates, not gospel.*

---

## 1. The strategic thesis

XVR (owned by **LearnPro Group**, PE-backed by Apiary Capital) is the incumbent — it supports nearly 100% of UK Fire & Rescue and serves 300+ organisations across 50+ countries with a hardware-heavy 3D VR platform. We are **not** trying to beat their 3D simulator head-on. The play is the opposite end of the market:

- **2D, browser-based, zero hardware** — runs from any laptop, classroom or meeting room.
- **Cheap and fast** — a flat licence at a fraction of a full VR deployment, live in a day.
- **Reuse-first** — build a scenario once (the digital TDE), run it hundreds of times.

**Why this market matters beyond blue-light — two strategic advantages:**

1. **The industrial / infrastructure sectors are mostly private companies.** That means *no public-procurement marathon* (no G-Cloud, no 6–12 month tenders) and — critically — *no conflict of interest with a fire-service employer*. Selling to an airport or chemical plant has zero overlap with public-sector employment. This sidesteps the single biggest legal risk.
2. **The training is legally mandated and recurring**, not discretionary (see Section 5). Airports, COMAH sites and others *must* run command/tabletop exercises on a fixed cycle and *evidence* them to a regulator. That is exactly what Command360 produces.

**Every organisation in Sections 3–4 has already decided to pay for command-training simulation.** They are the warmest possible leads for a cheaper alternative or a complementary tool.

## 4. Sector-by-sector target map

### 4.1 Fire & Rescue (UK)
- **Targets:** 49 UK fire & rescue services (44 England · 1 Scotland · 3 Wales · 1 NI). Plus the Fire Service College and regional training hubs.
- **Buyer / contact:** Head of Training & Development · Incident Command Lead · Learning & Organisational Development.
- **Buying trigger:** Mandated incident-command competence and assessment at operational/tactical/strategic levels; ongoing re-validation.
- **Scenarios they need:** structural fires (residential, high-rise, commercial), wildfire/rural, HAZMAT, RTC, BESS (battery storage) fires, fire investigation, control-room/dispatch, major incident command.
- **Reality:** XVR owns this space. Approach as the cheap, fast, browser-based supplement for the day-to-day TDE workflow and for services that can't justify a full VR suite. Hardest sector to win; best for proof and references.

### 4.2 Police & Security (UK)
- **Targets:** 45 territorial forces + National Crime Agency, British Transport Police, Civil Nuclear Constabulary, MOD Police, College of Policing.
- **Buyer / contact:** Head of Learning & Development · Public Order training lead · Command training / Firearms command.
- **Buying trigger:** JESIP multi-agency interoperability, public-order/command training, control-room decision-making.
- **Scenarios:** public disorder/crowd control, armed response, event security, JESIP major incidents, control-room with evolving intelligence, scene/evidence management.
- **Note:** Command-training adoption is younger than fire — more greenfield. *Watch:* a 2026 white paper proposes merging E&W forces, which may consolidate buyers.

### 4.3 Ambulance & Health (UK)
- **Targets:** 14 ambulance organisations (10 England trusts + Isle of Wight + Scotland + Wales + NI), NHS England, health academies, HART teams.
- **Buyer / contact:** Resilience / EPRR (Emergency Preparedness, Resilience & Response) lead · Clinical education.
- **Buying trigger:** Major-incident and mass-casualty (MTA) preparedness, EPRR requirements.
- **Scenarios:** mass-casualty triage, RTC with multiple casualties, command & control for ambulance leadership, control-room/dispatch, public-event surge.

### 4.4 Aviation — Airports (UK) ★ priority cold-market
- **Targets:** ~40 UK commercial airports, each with an in-house Rescue & Fire Fighting Service (RFFS); plus NATS and the International Fire Training Centre (Teesside).
- **Buyer / contact:** RFFS Station/Watch Manager · Airport Fire Training Officer · Head of Airside Safety.
- **Buying trigger (strong):** CAA **CAP 168** mandates the RFFS; **CAP 699** mandates an ongoing training and proficiency-check programme. Re-qualification is required. Training is non-negotiable and audited.
- **Scenarios:** aircraft ground emergencies, fuel/engine fires, terminal incidents, mass-casualty, off-airport response.
- **Why now:** private operators (fast sales), a clear named buyer, a regulator demanding evidence, and XVR has already proven airports buy this (Heathrow, Changi, Auckland, Ottawa).

### 4.5 Energy & Utilities (UK)
- **Targets:** oil refineries (~6: e.g. Stanlow, Fawley, Pembroke, Humber, Lindsey, Grangemouth) · North Sea oil & gas operators · nuclear (EDF stations, Sellafield, Hinkley/Sizewell new-build) · electricity distribution networks (~6 operator groups) · the 4 gas networks · ~13 water & sewerage companies.
- **Buyer / contact:** Head of HSE / Process Safety · Emergency Response Manager · Site Training Manager · Resilience lead.
- **Buying trigger:** Many sites are COMAH (see 4.6); plus sector safety cases, control-room competence and business-continuity drills.
- **Scenarios:** plant fires, gas/chemical release, BESS & substation fires, offshore platform incidents, network/outage crisis management, control-room exercises.

### 4.6 Major-Hazard Industrial — COMAH ★ largest cold-market pool
- **Targets:** ~950 COMAH establishments across Great Britain — chemical plants, petrochemical, fuel and gas storage, explosives, warehousing. Several hundred are **upper-tier**.
- **Buyer / contact:** Site Safety / Process Safety Manager · Emergency Planning Officer · Training Coordinator.
- **Buying trigger (very strong):** COMAH requires major-accident prevention and emergency planning; **upper-tier sites must test their emergency plans at least every three years using a multi-agency tabletop exercise** against a credible worst-case scenario — and document it. That mandated, repeated tabletop *is* the Command360 use case.
- **Scenarios:** tank-farm fire, vapour-cloud/BLEVE, toxic release, loss of containment, multi-agency on-site command, business continuity.
- **Why now:** huge volume, private, regulated, and currently served by either nothing structured or kit that's overkill. The "build once, reuse, auto-generate the regulator's exercise record" pitch is decisive here.

## 5. Buying-trigger cheat sheet (lead with these)

The fastest way into a cold account is to anchor on the rule that forces them to train:

- **Airports** → CAP 168 (RFFS required) + CAP 699 (mandatory ongoing training & proficiency checks; periodic re-qualification).
- **COMAH upper-tier sites** → external emergency plan must be **tested every 3 years** via multi-agency tabletop, and documented.
- **Fire & Rescue** → incident-command competence framework; operational/tactical/strategic assessment.
- **Police** → JESIP (joint multi-agency interoperability) + command and public-order training.
- **Ambulance / health** → EPRR (Emergency Preparedness, Resilience & Response) major-incident standards.
- **Rail / transport** → safety case + ORR; station/tunnel emergency plans.
- **Ports / maritime** → Port Marine Safety Code + COMAH for fuel/chemical terminals.

Pitch line that works across all of them: *"You already have to run these command exercises and prove you did. Build each one once in the browser, re-run it as often as you like, and walk away with the after-action record your regulator wants — no hardware, no VR suite."*

## Appendix A — Product specialisms to build into Command360

The core engine does **not** need to change much per sector — that's the advantage of the 2D-overlay approach. What changes is mostly **content** (asset libraries + scenario templates) plus a handful of framework features that map directly to the buying triggers. Priority order:

**Tier 1 — highest leverage, lowest engineering**
1. **Sector content packs (asset + scenario libraries).** The biggest differentiator and mostly an AI-asset effort, not deep code. Build a transparent-layer asset bank and ready-made scenario templates per vertical:
   - *Aviation:* aircraft types, runway/apron, terminal, fuel & engine fires.
   - *COMAH/industrial:* tank farms, pipework, chemical plant, vapour cloud / BLEVE, toxic release.
   - *Energy:* substations, BESS fires, offshore platforms, wind turbines, refineries.
   - *Rail:* rolling stock, tunnels, level crossings, overhead lines, stations.
   - *Maritime:* vessels, quayside, port infrastructure.
2. **Regulator-ready debrief / after-action export.** Extend Debrief mode to auto-generate a timestamped exercise record: decisions made, timeline, participant performance vs objectives, exportable to **PDF and spreadsheet**. This is the feature that closes COMAH and aviation deals, because they must *evidence* the exercise.
3. **Per-organisation branding / white-label.** Org-scoped logo, colours and asset library (rides on the multi-tenancy work). Each client wants their own uniforms, crests and local scenes.

**Tier 2 — maps to the buying trigger**
4. **Command-role & framework structure.** Support operational/tactical/strategic (bronze/silver/gold) command roles, a **JESIP-aligned decision log**, and objectives tagged to the relevant competence framework (fire command, CAP 699 for RFFS, COMAH exercise objectives). Lets a training manager tick regulatory boxes. Builds on existing Tasking/Objectives.
5. **Multi-agency / external-participant live sessions.** Guest/observer roles and multi-org sessions so a site can pull in the local fire/police/ambulance partners remotely via a join link or the planned QR second-screen. A genuine edge over single-site 3D installs.

**Tier 3 — broadens applicability**
6. **Control-room / dispatch scenario mode.** A lighter scenario type driven by incoming calls/intel injects (text/audio) rather than a full visual scene — explicitly requested across police, ambulance, rail and energy.
7. **LMS / competency export hooks.** Optional export or integration so results can feed an existing competency system (mirrors how LearnPro cross-sells eFireService/pdrPro).

**Gating requirement (not a feature, but needed to sell):**
8. **Security & compliance posture.** Cyber Essentials, SSO, audit logs, data-retention controls, clear data-residency. Public bodies and large industrials will ask; XVR is ISO 27001. Have answers ready before serious conversations.

**The reassuring takeaway:** roughly 80% of serving these verticals is content (assets + templates), which suits the AI-generated-layer approach and scales cheaply. The genuine engineering additions — debrief/evidence export, command-role tagging, multi-agency live — are bounded and mostly extend features already on the roadmap.
