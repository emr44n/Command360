The one to take

Call them Injects, and the whole product clicks into place

"The instructor injects events as the candidate makes decisions" — that's the literal language of exercise design. Your live board becomes Injects ready to fire; the build screen becomes the Inject builder; the scenario is a library of injects. It's distinct from XVR, it's authentic to the job, and nobody needs it explained.

Looks — XVR's "States", reimagined

One layer, many looks — and you tweak them live.

In XVR a "State" is a baked snapshot. You can do better: a Look is a layer's saved visual treatment — colour, contrast, brightness, blur, a filter — applied in real time, so it's endlessly tweakable and costs nothing to re-render. Save a few looks per layer (or per whole scene) and switch them with a step.

Where AI comes in: filters handle colour and mood instantly, but for a true structural variant — the same building damaged, the same room flooded, the same person injured — generate that look once at authoring time with AI, save it as a Look, and switch to it live. XVR could never do this; you can, because you're working with images, not fixed 3D models.

The firefighter feature

Thermal view — the one only a fire service asks for.

XVR has heat sources, heat containers and a temperature slider per object, all visible only in thermal-camera mode. Translated to 2D, it's genuinely simple — and a standout selling point, because a TIC (thermal imaging camera) view is core to modern firefighting and almost nobody's flat tool offers it.

🌡

How it looks

Tap thermal and the scene flips to a heat view — the base goes cool and inverted, fire and bodies glow white-hot, cooled areas read dark. Exactly your instinct: a negative-style base with warm highlights on top.

◉

How it's built

Cheap way: a real-time filter on the scene (invert + heat gradient) plus warm radial-glow PNGs over the hot bits. Crisp way: a pre-rendered thermal version of the plate as a Look. Each layer carries a heat value so it knows how to glow.

◎

How it's used

A Thermal step flips the whole scene; or a Hotspot lets the candidate "raise the camera" on a chosen area — click the door, see the heat bleeding through it. Place heat sources in an inject as the fire develops.

Pop-ups, done properly

A Card to show something. A Question to branch on it.

XVR's two pop-up actions are richer than they first look, and both translate beautifully. A Card targets who sees it and how it's laid out; a Question puts choices to the candidate where each answer fires its own inject — which is your branching, self-led-training engine in one feature.

🖼 Card

Push an image, a video (file or YouTube), text, or a mix onto the screen. Set the audience (just the candidate · everyone · only the instructor), the layout, and entrance / dwell / exit (fade · slide · cut). Add a link to open a document. The candidate reacts; you close it when the point lands. Perfect for a casualty close-up, a hazmat label, body-cam footage, a map.

☑ Question branching

Show a multiple-choice prompt (single or multi-select, up to 8 options, optional image). The hidden power: each answer chains to a different inject. "Offensive" plays one path; "Defensive" plays another. Runs instructor-led, or fully self-led with no instructor present — the candidate's choices drive the whole scenario. Your voteOptions + branch-manager already do most of this.

The build screen, 2026

Big setup on the left. Whatever you click, its details on the right.

Exactly the layout you described, built the way modern tools are: a left rail for the big moves (Library, Layers, Injects, Atmosphere), the Stage in the middle, and a context Inspector on the right that always shows the settings of whatever's selected. The Library uses real thumbnails with hover-preview — grid or list — not XVR's flat icon list.

Object toolbar

Select a layer and the top bar gives you show · lock · disable · duplicate · delete · order — the operations XVR buries in menus, one click away.

Where to start

Lock the names, then build one inject end to end.

Decide your words from the menu — I'd take Inject · Step · link-to-play-together · Wait · Phase · Look · Tasking · Hotspot. Then wire the link toggle and a Wait step, add Reveal / Hide / Card / Question, and author "fire breaks through the front window". That one inject proves the engine, the Looks, and the live loop — and most of it is already in your code.
