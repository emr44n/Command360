# 01 · Product Overview & Positioning

## 1. What it is

Command 360 is a **browser-based, 2D, AI-assisted incident-command training and assessment platform**. An instructor builds a scene from a flat backplate (a photo of a building, street, site) and layers transparent effects, characters, and media on top. Participants experience it live in a browser; the instructor drives it and voices the characters over a video call (Teams).

It is deliberately **flat (2D), not 3D**. The bet: most command training is about **decisions, communication, and situational awareness**, not photorealistic 3D navigation. 2D is faster to build, cheaper to run, works on any device including iPad, and needs no hardware install.

## 2. Who it's for

- **Primary:** UK fire & rescue services (the founder's domain).
- **Then:** police, ambulance, aviation/RFFS, energy & utilities, COMAH major-hazard, rail, maritime/ports, defence.
- **Buyer:** training managers / command trainers in those organisations.
- **End users:** instructors (build & run) and participants (command-level officers — roughly Crew Commander and above).

## 3. Positioning — against XVR On Scene

XVR On Scene (XVR Simulation, part of LearnPro Group) is the UK incumbent: a 3D virtual-reality command simulator, sold per-PC-licence at thousands of pounds, requiring install.

| | XVR On Scene | Command 360 |
|---|---|---|
| Format | 3D VR sim | 2D layered scenes |
| Install | Desktop install | Browser, nothing to install |
| Device | High-spec PC | Any device incl. iPad |
| Build speed | Slow, 3D scene work | Fast, drop a photo + overlays |
| Pricing | ~£2,500/PC, 2-licence min | Flat site licence (~£4k–£28k by org size) |
| Remote/multi-agency | Hard | Native (browser join link / QR) |

**The wedge:** cheaper than VR, browser-based, no hardware, multi-agency by default, with a regulator-ready evidence record.

### Why not a $75 tool (e.g. SimsUShare)?
Those are **solo sim builders** for one person on a desktop. Command 360 is a **live, remote, multi-participant assessment platform** with an authoring studio, a shared library, classroom delivery, and an evidence trail. Different product class.

## 4. What it is / what it is NOT

**It IS:**
- A **creation tool** (Command Studio builds the assets and scenarios).
- A **delivery tool** (Classroom for taught groups, Live for immersive crew assessment).
- A **record keeper** (Debrief produces the evidence export).

**It is NOT:**
- A competency-**marking** system. It doesn't auto-grade officers against a framework. (An optional objectives/scoring layer can be added later; partners like Effective Command own accredited marking.)
- A 3D simulator.
- A talking-AI-avatar product. Characters are **pre-rendered loops voiced live by the instructor**.

## 5. The value proposition (the sentences that sell)

- "Build a realistic command exercise from a single photo, in minutes, in your browser."
- "Run it live with your crew anywhere — no hardware, no install."
- "Walk away with a branded after-action record you can hand to the regulator."
- "Your NOG, your way" — fully configurable to each service's own framework.

## 6. Commercial frame (context, not a build requirement)

- **Pricing:** four-band flat site licence by establishment size (operational staff + station count) — Small £4k / Standard £7k / Large £12k / National £18k / Scotland bespoke £22–28k. Always priced below the XVR equivalent. Quoted ex-VAT.
- **Privacy-by-design** is a procurement advantage: participants hold no accounts and (by default) no personal data; decision logs auto-wipe.
- **Exit thesis:** £2–3m via ARR growth or strategic acquisition (LearnPro has a roll-up pattern).
