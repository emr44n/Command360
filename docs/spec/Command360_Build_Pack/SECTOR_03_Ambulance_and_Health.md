# Sector 03 — Ambulance & Health

*The third blue-light service, governed by a single national framework (NHS EPRR) with an annual assurance process that forces evidenced readiness. Smaller buyer count but each is large. XVR already sells here (NHS England, South Western, Yorkshire). The hook is the same as fire and police: mandated command competence + an annual assurance return that needs evidence.*

---

## 1. Snapshot
- **Target universe:** 14 ambulance organisations (10 England trusts + Isle of Wight + the Scottish, Welsh and NI services), plus acute trusts with major-incident/EPRR duties, integrated care boards, and NHS England regional EPRR teams.
- **Procurement:** Public sector (NHS frameworks); slow but a clear annual driver.
- **Buyer / contact:** Head of EPRR / Resilience · EPRR Training Lead · Accountable Emergency Officer (board-level) · HART/SORT leads · Emergency Operations Centre (EOC) management.
- **Edge over incumbent:** commanders need *annual* assurance updates and JESIP familiarisation; a browser tool runs command and mass-casualty exercises for dispersed staff and EOCs, and produces the assurance evidence.

## 2. The mandate (the rigid guidelines)
- **NHS England EPRR Framework + Core Standards for EPRR** — minimum requirements assessed through an **annual assurance process** (self-assessment + scoring). Statutory under the **Civil Contingencies Act 2004** (Category 1 responder), **NHS Act 2006** and **Health and Care Act 2022**.
- **Command levels: Operational / Tactical / Strategic** — defined in the framework; Strategic and Tactical Commanders maintain **decision logs**; commanders receive annual updates and assurance.
- **Minimum Occupational Standards for EPRR** — for commanders, command support and EPRR staff (Skills for Justice National Occupational Standards underpin CPD).
- **Principles of Health Command (PoHC)** — the strategic-leader course (delivered to 1,600+ a year) plus a newer tactical course; **Loggist** training package.
- **Specialist capabilities with their own standards:** HART (Hazardous Area Response Team — inner cordon/hot zone), SORT (Special Operations Response Team — CBRN/HAZMAT clinical decontamination), MTA (Marauding Terrorist Attack) response, MERIT (Medical Emergency Response Incident Team).
- **METHANE** alerting; **JESIP** (annual familiarisation for all operational/EOC staff); the **Mass Casualty Concept of Operations** and Manchester Arena Inquiry recommendations (e.g. event healthcare).

## 3. Competencies to develop (worked backwards from EPRR)
- **Incident command** at operational/tactical/strategic level, with decision logging.
- **Mass-casualty management & triage** — casualty clearing, prioritisation, conveyance and receiving-hospital coordination.
- **METHANE alerting** and information flow to control and partners.
- **JESIP joint working** — co-location, METHANE, JDM with fire and police.
- **Specialist response command** — directing HART in the hot zone, SORT decontamination, MTA/CBRN response.
- **EOC / control-room operation** — resource tasking, mutual aid, SITREPs.
- **Business continuity & recovery**, and loggist/decision-capture discipline.

## 4. What they train on (scenarios)
Mass-casualty "big bang" · road traffic collision / multi-vehicle · marauding terrorist attack · CBRN / HAZMAT with decontamination · building collapse / entrapment · major transport accident · EOC / control-room exercise · pandemic / surge · mutual-aid and multi-agency command.

## 5. How Command360 helps (competency → capability — SEO copy seed)
- **Command at O/T/S** → run the incident live; the Ambulance Incident Commander tasks resources and the scene develops; replay for assurance.
- **Mass-casualty triage** → place casualties of varying priority on the 2D scene; practise triage sieve/sort, casualty clearing and conveyance decisions; hotspots to triage and tag.
- **METHANE & JESIP** → built-in METHANE builder and multi-agency sessions that bring fire and police commanders in remotely.
- **Specialist teams** → HART hot-zone and SORT decontamination scenarios without standing up a live exercise.
- **Annual assurance evidence** → auto-generated exercise record (decisions, participants, objectives vs Core Standards) exported to PDF/spreadsheet — the document the EPRR return needs.

- **Standards body:** NHS England EPRR (Framework, Core Standards, PoHC, Loggist package); the Association of Ambulance Chief Executives (AACE).
- **Delivery:** trust EPRR teams · PoHC strategic/tactical courses · **MIMMS** (Major Incident Medical Management and Support, ALSG) · HART national training · JESIP joint training.
