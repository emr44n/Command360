# Sector 06 — Major-Hazard Industrial (COMAH)

*The largest cold-market pool and arguably the best-fit for Command360. ~950 sites, mostly private, a hard regulatory trigger (a tested exercise every three years that must be documented), and currently served by either nothing structured or kit that's overkill. The "build once, reuse, walk away with the regulator's exercise record" pitch is decisive here.*

---

## 1. Snapshot
- **Target universe:** ~950 COMAH establishments across Great Britain — chemical plants, petrochemical, oil & gas processing, fuel & gas storage (tank farms), explosives, hazardous-substance warehousing. Several hundred are **upper-tier** (the heaviest duties).
- **Procurement:** **Private** — direct, fast. No public-tender marathon, no employer conflict.
- **Buyer / contact:** Site Safety / Process Safety Manager · Emergency Planning Officer · HSE Manager · Training Coordinator · (and the **Local Resilience Forum** that owns the off-site/external plan for upper-tier sites).
- **Why it fits:** these sites run mandated multi-agency tabletop exercises and must evidence them — exactly Command360's job, and the on-site fire/emergency teams need recurring command practice they can't easily get live.

## 2. The mandate (the rigid guidelines)
The clearest buying trigger in this whole knowledge base:
- **COMAH 2015 (Control of Major Accident Hazards Regulations)** — implements the EU Seveso III Directive; enforced by the **Competent Authority** (HSE/ONR + environment regulator).
- **Regulation 4 — general duty:** take all measures necessary to prevent major accidents and limit consequences.
- **Major Accident Prevention Policy (MAPP)** and an **internal emergency plan** for all sites; upper-tier sites also produce a full **safety report** and an **external emergency plan** (with the local authority/LRF).
- **The trigger:** upper-tier external emergency plans **must be tested at least every three years** — in practice via an **activity-based multi-agency tabletop exercise** against a **credible worst-case scenario** drawn from the safety report — and the results reviewed and documented.
- **Competence underpinning:** process safety competence and emergency-response readiness are expected throughout (HSE inspects against this).

## 3. Competencies to develop (worked backwards from COMAH)
- **On-site incident command** — initial response, declaring/escalating, command of site emergency response teams and works fire crews.
- **Emergency plan activation** — internal then external plan, mobilising mutual aid and blue-light services, site-to-LRF coordination.
- **Major-accident scenario management** — fire, explosion, toxic/flammable release, loss of containment; protecting people on and off site.
- **Risk & consequence awareness** — recognising escalation (e.g. domino effects, BLEVE, vapour-cloud), shelter-in-place vs evacuate decisions.
- **Multi-agency / JESIP coordination** — joint command with fire/police/ambulance, METHANE, Joint Decision Model, off-site impact and public protection.
- **Control-room / emergency-control-centre operation** — running the site ECC, information flow, status boards.
- **Business continuity & recovery** — containment, environmental protection (firewater run-off — the Buncefield lesson), restart.
- **Communication** — with responders, regulator, neighbouring sites and the public.

## 4. What they train on (scenarios)
Tank-farm / bund fire · vapour-cloud release & ignition · BLEVE / pressure-vessel failure · toxic gas release with off-site plume · loss of containment / pipeline rupture · loading/transfer incident · warehouse chemical fire · multi-agency on-site command exercise · ECC / control-room exercise · business-continuity & recovery exercise.

## 5. How Command360 helps (competency → capability — SEO copy seed)
- **The mandated 3-yearly tabletop, digitised** → build the site's credible-worst-case once, run it as an interactive multi-agency exercise, re-run annually with variations. Replaces the static paper/PowerPoint tabletop.
- **On-site command** → live scene of the plant; trigger release/fire; site IC commits teams and the incident evolves with decisions.
- **Multi-agency coordination** → bring the LRF, local fire/police/ambulance into one browser session remotely — no one travels.
- **Consequence / shelter decisions** → injects showing plume spread or escalation, forcing shelter-vs-evacuate calls; hotspots to identify hazards.
- **The evidence pack** → auto-generated exercise record (timeline, decisions, participants, objectives met) exportable to PDF/spreadsheet — *the document the Competent Authority wants to see.* This is the feature that wins COMAH accounts.
- **Site-specific realism** → the bespoke asset onboarding pack recreates *their* plant, tanks and layout, so the exercise is recognisably their site.

## 7. Training landscape (providers, awarding bodies, courses)
- **Standards/bodies:** HSE / Competent Authority guidance · IChemE (process safety) · IOSH/NEBOSH (safety) · EEMUA (engineering) · industry mutual-aid schemes.
- **Training providers:** specialist process-safety & emergency-response trainers, fire training colleges with industrial/petrochemical modules, private industrial fire-service providers (e.g. **Falck**, and joint industrial brigades like **Gezamenlijke Brandweer** at major port/industrial complexes), emergency-planning consultancies that design and run the exercises.
- **Incumbent simulation:** XVR (Phillips 66 refining; energy/transport clients) at the high end. *Command360's lane: affordable, browser, the recurring mandated tabletop + the HSE evidence pack — for the many sites that can't justify a VR suite.*
