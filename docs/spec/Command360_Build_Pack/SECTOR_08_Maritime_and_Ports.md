# Sector 08 — Maritime & Ports

*A private/trust-port pool under a code whose compliance is reported to the MCA every three years and is "not optional" for harbour authorities. Emergency plans must be "well-practised", and oil-spill exercises are run routinely — a clean recurring-exercise driver. XVR already sells adjacent here (RNLI).*

---

## 1. Snapshot
- **Target universe:** statutory harbour authorities and major port groups (Port of London Authority, Associated British Ports, Peel Ports, Forth Ports, Dover, Felixstowe, Portsmouth, etc.), plus marinas, terminals and ferry operators; many oil/chemical terminals are *also* COMAH (Sector 06).
- **Procurement:** Mostly **private / trust ports** — direct and relatively fast.
- **Buyer / contact:** Harbour Master · Designated Person · Marine Safety / Emergency Planning Manager · Port HSE · (ferry) Operations.
- **Edge:** the three-yearly compliance cycle and routine oil-spill / emergency exercises are exactly what a browser exercise tool can run and evidence — and ports have dispersed staff and partner agencies a remote-join session suits.

## 2. The mandate (the rigid guidelines)
- **Port Marine Safety Code (PMSC)** — updated April 2025 as the **Ports & Marine Facilities Safety Code (PMFSC)**, published by the **Maritime and Coastguard Agency (MCA)**; **compliance is reported to the MCA every three years** and is not optional for harbour authorities.
- **Roles:** **Duty Holder** (accountable for safe marine operations), **Designated Person** (independent assurance and **audit** of the safety management system, with direct access to the Duty Holder), **Harbour Master** (operations, and often responsible for emergency plans and dangerous goods).
- **Marine Safety Management System (MSMS)** built on a **Formal Safety Assessment (FSA)** with risks reduced to **ALARP**; emergency plans must be "appropriate and well-practised".
- **Civil Contingencies Act 2004** — ports are frequently **Category 2 responders** and LRF members.
- **MAIB** (Marine Accident Investigation Branch) recommendations feed practice; **JESIP** applies to multi-agency response.

## 3. Competencies to develop (worked backwards from the Code)
- **Marine incident command** — Harbour Master-led response, escalation, coordination across the harbour and approaches.
- **Emergency-plan activation** — declaring and running the port emergency plan and Marine Emergency Centre.
- **Pollution / spill response** — oil and chemical spill management (National Contingency Plan / OPRC), shoreline and environmental protection.
- **Vessel firefighting & rescue coordination** — at berth, at anchor and in the approaches; man-overboard / water rescue.
- **Dangerous-goods management** — vessels carrying hazardous cargo in transit.
- **Multi-agency & Coastguard coordination** — with HM Coastguard, RNLI, fire/police/ambulance and salvage.
- **VTS / control-room operation**, business continuity and recovery.

## 4. What they train on (scenarios)
Vessel fire (at berth / anchor) · collision or grounding · oil / chemical spill · dangerous-goods incident · man overboard / water rescue · passenger-ferry evacuation · quayside / cargo incident · terminal fire (COMAH overlap) · major pollution event.
